# Bharat Herald: Print Decline and Digital Transition Analysis

## Project Overview

Bharat Herald, a national newspaper, has experienced a steady decline in print circulation and advertising revenue between 2019 and 2024. With changing reader behavior and growing digital media consumption, the organization is exploring a phased digital relaunch.

This project analyzes print circulation trends, advertising revenue patterns, and digital readiness across cities to identify key business challenges and provide data-driven recommendations for Bharat Herald’s digital transition strategy.

---

## Business Questions

The analysis focuses on answering the following questions:

1. How has print circulation changed between 2019 and 2024?
2. Are return rates improving or worsening over time?
3. Which cities generate the highest circulation and advertising revenue?
4. How has advertising revenue evolved across different ad categories?
5. Is there a relationship between city-level circulation and advertising revenue?
6. Which cities show high digital readiness but low digital engagement?
7. Which cities should be prioritized for Bharat Herald’s digital relaunch?

## Key Business Insights

- Print circulation has steadily declined between 2019 and 2024, confirming a structural shift toward digital media consumption.
- Distribution efficiency remains stable, as indicated by relatively constant return rates.
- A limited number of cities drive a large portion of circulation and ad revenue.
- Advertising performance varies significantly across categories.
- Several cities show strong digital readiness but limited digital engagement, suggesting untapped growth opportunities.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

1. Prioritize digital rollout in cities with high digital readiness and strong circulation bases.

2. Introduce mobile-friendly content formats such as optimized e-papers and short-form digital news.

3. Offer bundled advertising packages combining print and digital channels.

4. Implement hybrid subscription models that combine print and digital access to ease the transition for existing readers.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis shows that while print circulation is declining, Bharat Herald maintains strong market presence in several cities. By leveraging digital readiness and targeted content strategies, the company can transition toward a sustainable digital-first model while preserving value from its existing print audience.
